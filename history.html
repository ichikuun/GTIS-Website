Land purchase was made and construction began for a new international school to be built in Gnor Village, Kampong Speu.
Before its current name, GTIS used to be known as Hope International Primary School (HIPS). It started with only 3 students and was the first school in the village to teach subjects outside the local curriculum.

The first premises comprised a 2-storey block with 4 classrooms, a principal’s office and a staff room. When the school first opened its doors, there were 4 teachers and 3 students.
At the old school site, student enrolment of 350 pupils caused the school to be bursting at its seams! God provided resources through a benefactor to finance the purchase of land and the construction of a new school complex that was 800 meters from the original school building.
HIPS is renamed Hope International Secondary School as it prepares for the first Grade 10 class. First group of students took the Grade 9 National Examinations of Cambodia Graduates, with 6 achieving outstanding state results.
GTIS was re-branded with a new school vision & mission, values, motto, crest, song and uniforms for students and staff. The new school complex is a 4-storey building with an assembly area, 14 classrooms, staff offices and a multi-purpose and training room.
In May 2014, the school was finally renamed Glad Tidings International School. The school now has a canteen with the auditorium above it, and a covered linkway connecting it with the main school block.
With the newly-built eco-garden, basketball and badminton courts, facilities were added to the school grounds to spice up student life and get them to enjoy the beautiful outdoors of Cambodia.
The first cohort of Grade 12 pupils sat for the National G12 Examinations of Cambodia. Meanwhile, in September, students and teachers went for a spiritual retreat and training with the school's renewed focus on 'Calling and Purpose'.
GTIS celebrated its 10th anniversary with the Deputy Governor of Kg Speu Province, with invited guests like past principals and trainers from Australia, Malaysia and Singapore.
The school operates from Grades 1 to 12 and has 498 pupils, 25 full time teachers and 5 support staff. G10 to G12 classes cease temporarily due to shortage of qualified teachers.

$500,000

The next ten years will pose different challenges. Due to rising affluence, the wages and maintenance costs would rise. We are facing a shortage of qualified teachers, and as a result, very sadly and reluctantly, we had to cease offering G10 to 12 classes, effective next semester.

We are currently short of classrooms, and we need a computer lab, a proper library and a science lab to ‘complete’ the school. Plans for a new adjoining building have been drawn up, and the total cost of construction and facilities is estimated at S$500,000.

Our History

The GTIS pedagogy comprises of assemblies, indoor and outdoor learning, sports and Christian education. Dedicated teachers and support staff strive to maximize the learning experience of each child. No effort is spared to inculcate biblical values and a love for lifelong learning.

2006

Land purchase was made and construction began for a new international school to be built in Gnor Village, Kampong Speu.

2007

Before its current name, GTIS used to be known as Hope International Primary School (HIPS). It started with only 3 students and was the first school in the village to teach subjects outside the local curriculum.

The first premises comprised a 2-storey block with 4 classrooms, a principal’s office and a staff room. When the school first opened its doors, there were 4 teachers and 3 students.

2011

At the old school site, student enrolment of 350 pupils caused the school to be bursting at its seams! God provided resources through a benefactor to finance the purchase of land and the construction of a new school complex 800 meters from the original school building.

2012

HIPS is renamed Hope International Secondary School as it prepares for the first Grade 10 class. First group of students took the Grade 9 National Examinations of Cambodia Graduates, with 6 achieving outstanding state results.

2013

GTIS was re-branded with a new school vision & mission, values, motto, crest, song and uniforms for students and staff. The new school complex is a 4-storey building with an assembly area, 14 classrooms, staff offices and a multi-purpose and training room.

2014

In May 2014, the school was finally renamed Glad Tidings International School. The school now has a canteen with the auditorium above it, and a covered linkway connecting it with the main school block.

2015

With the newly-built eco-garden, basketball and badminton courts, facilities were added to the school grounds to spice up student life and get them to enjoy the beautiful outdoors of Cambodia.

2016

The first cohort of Grade 12 pupils sat for the National G12 Examinations of Cambodia. Meanwhile, in September, students and teachers went for a spiritual retreat and training with the school's renewed focus on 'Calling and Purpose'.

2017

GTIS celebrated its 10th anniversary with the Deputy Governor of Kg Speu Province, with invited guests like past principals and trainers from Australia, Malaysia and Singapore.
The school operates from Grades 1 to 12 and has 498 pupils, 25 full time teachers and 5 support staff. G10 to G12 classes cease temporarily due to shortage of qualified teachers.
