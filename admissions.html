Admissions

Thank you for your interest in GTIS. We recognize the importance of finding the right educational setting for your family. We are here to help. From first contact to the first day of school, our friendly Admissions team will answer all your questions and guide you through the enrollment and transition process. We encourage you to explore our website to find out more about our school, our academic and extracurricular programs, and to come for a visit to see our students in action.

How to apply

Once you have decided that a GTIS education can benefit your child, you can take the first step in the application process. We promise a straightforward and easy procedure.

Step 1: Download an Application Form here. Note that in Cambodia, the Academic Year starts in October and the application period will be in May. Submit the completed softcopy to admissions@gtis.edu.kh.

Step 2: Scan and email ALL required documents online. Please ensure all files are appropriately labelled.







Step 3: Pay a non-refundable Application Fee of US$50 once an Application Invoice is generated and sent to you.

Step 4: We will inform you if we need your child to come down for an English test or an interview with one of our Senior Teachers.

Step 5: The outcome of the application would be released to you via email.

Edcation Requirements

Primary
Min. 6 years of age by October 1 AND
Completed a pre-school programme OR Pass an Early Years aptitude test

Secondary
Min. 12 years of age by October 1 AND
Completed Grades 1-6 in another primary school OR Possess an official elementary school completion certificate

High
Min. 15 years of age by October 1 AND
Completed Grade 9 in another secondary school OR Possess an official middle school completion certificate OR Possess a national examination transcript (Grade 9 or equivalent)

School Fees

Once your child has been accepted, we would need to receive full fee payment for the first semester before commencement of the Academic Year.

GTIS is a non-profit organization that relies on fees and donations to upkeep its operations. Fees are reviewed by the School Board biannually during its Budget meeting. Parents/ legal guardians are responsible for ensuring fees are paid on time, once per semester.
